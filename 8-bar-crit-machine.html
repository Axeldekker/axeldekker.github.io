8bar's new crit machine is out and it's SICK!

by Vlad Pacala 28 March 2018

With the first Rad Race of the year and the Berliner Fahrradschau happening in the same weekend, 8bar found the perfect opportunity to officially release the newest addition to their collection: the TMPLHOF. Keeping with their tradition of naming bikes after certain areas in Berlin, this one is named after the famous Tempelhof Airport, which is also the location of 8bar’s annual crit.

The TMPLHOF is their new team bike, made for performance. We’ll be seeing this one at all the major crits this year. First thing that catches your eye, they gave up the old KRZBERG low-pro geometry in favor of a sloped frame for a lighter setup.

As we’ve seen before, their allegiance to DT Swiss wheels remains strong. The bike comes with their high-end Track TRC 1400 DICUT wheels paired with Schwalbe Pro One tubeless tires.

This time, 8bar paired up with Brooks to make a special edition, custom Cambium C13 saddle in the team colors which makes for the perfect match.

This handlebar has attracted everyone’s attention so 8bar came with the explanation: they are an internal prototype of their standard 8bar GIGA handlebars with custom 3D printed “aero hoods” which make the geometry of the bars look the way it does.

The bike proved itself at RAD RACE, with two 8bar team members landing in the finals. You can read more about it here.

There is no precise info yet, but 8bar mentioned on their Instagram account that the starting price of the bike will be around 1.350,- Euro and will be available to buy starting in May.
